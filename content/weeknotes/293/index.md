    I'm really in a DIY mood so once we got home I started buying the equipment I needed to resurface our kitchen worktop. It's solid wood, which is great, but the previous owner had varnished it and it was getting pretty worse for wear. This is going to be a boring description of a job I'm now really proud of.

    After doing a lot of reading _and_ talking to my kitchen fitter uncle, I decided the best thing to do would be to sand it down and oil it instead of revarnishing. I'll need to reapply oil every 6 months to a year _but_ I can patch repair it if needed instead of having to sand it down again.

	So I sanded the whole thing down multiple times at different grits. I love my new orbital sander :+1: Getting the varnish and silicone off was a real pain in the ass despite my tools and I found that careful use of a wood chisel worked better than "official" silicone removal tools.

    There was a patch of rot beside the sink because water often pools in the area around the tap. This is because the tap is set into the worktop instead of the sink itself and the varnish has been degrading for years. I chipped away the worst of it, used a wood hardener to stop it rotting further, and then some wood filler to patch over the gaps. For a first try I'm quite pleased with it! I'm also pleased that the unexpected setback didn't take the wind out of my sails.

    Today I applied the first coat of oil, I'm using Osmo Top Oil (Terra Satin) because it's a little lower maintenance and I wanted a dark oak tint. Another coat tomorrow then I can hopefully do the caulking on Tuesday :tada: anyway if you made is this far you're more patient than I would be.
