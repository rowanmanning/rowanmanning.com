  * I've had an absolutely wild week of ups and downs. I'm emotionally and physically exhausted.

  * We kicked off with my dad's birthday. He would have been 78 this year if he wasn't dead. I knew him for 18 years and he's been dead for 20 so it's weird coming to terms with the fact I probably know some of my friends better than I knew him. It's also weird thinking about the fact that the "me" he knew is very much not the person I am today, would we even get on? Who knows. Weird.

  * On Thursday I gave a well received talk at work. I know it was well received because a dozen people DMed me directly about it to say so, that's never happened before. Also the head of our department kept nodding at me a lot while I was speaking. I was given 24 hours to prepare the talk so I think I can call it a success. I _nearly_ burned all the goodwill that I generated by asking why we have no budget for promotions but decided to bank that credit for now instead.

  * After the talk I left the office because I had a half day booked. It wasn't a fun half day, I was driving to visit my little brother in hospital.

      George, who's 29, is going through chemotherapy at the moment. It's been a lot, it's causing a lot of stress for the family, it's causing a lot of stress for me. He's responding well but, you know, cancer is the thing that killed my dad so it's hard not to go there.

  * While I was north of London with the car I dropped into Grandma's and mum popped over. We mostly talked about George but also the house.

I spent the first half of the day doing a few house tasks for the flat, I took apart all the waste pipes for the kitchen sink and washing machine and gave them a deep clean, they were absolutely rancid so I felt like I achieved something.
